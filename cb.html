Shri Chunilal Bhaichand Metha
Jain Vidyalaya (Hostel).
Wadala, Mumbai

20th Century was the period when Mumbai was growing by leaps and bounds.
It was becoming financial capital of our country and also top most educational hub having prestigious colleges of various branches and university.
Meritorious students from small towns of Gujarat, Rajasthan and Maharashtra wanted to pursue their higher studies in Mumbai but biggest problem was accommodation to stay in Mumbai.
Dreams of hundreds of students must not have been fulfilled due to this problem.
There was a dire need for hostel specially for Sthanakvasi Jain Students coming to Mumbai.

Looking at such issue visionary person like Chimanlal Chakubhai Shah, Durlabhajibhai Khetani and others had dream to start a hostel in Mumbai.

The dream became reality with generous support of Lady Tapibai Chunilal Mehta and thus Sir C B Mehta Jain Vidhyalay came into existence in the year 1943 with capacity of 40 students at Adi Marzaban Road in Fort area of Mumbai.

Hundreds of meritorious students made their excellent career with the support of this hostel.
As a part of its expansion project, the same hostel was shifted in a new building adjacent to K R Sangharajaka hostel in Wadala in 1972.
The new hostel had now capacity for 75 students with better facilities.
Knowledge is power and this hostel was a power house for the students for whom sky was the limit.
