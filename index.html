The Jain Education Society

Established in 1943
Registered under Society's Registration act XXI of 1860
and
Registered under the Bombay Public Trust Act, XXIX of 1950

The Jain Education Society, Mumbai was founded in 1943 by Late Shri Chimanbhai Chakubhai Shah, Solicitor & Member of Constitutional Assembly and Shri Durlabhjibhai Khetani, Industrialist & Freedom fighter.

In the initial years, its objective was to provide hostel accommodation to the bright students who were coming to Mumbai from their native place in Gujarat, Rajasthan & interiors of Maharashtra, for pursuing higher education in several branches including Medicine, Engineering, Science, C.A., Liberal Arts, Architecture etc, deserving students were given scholarships and liberal financial assistance to enable them to pursue their higher education. So far approximately 4000 students are benifitted.

This pre-independence Charatible organization has been contributing significantly in the field of education for the students of Jain community.

JES ran Gujrati Medium School at Chirabazar, Mumbai for more than 30 years.(Ratna chintamani school)

Currently running 3 boys hostels, school at Rajkot and various other education assisting programes, JES is enormously contributing to the progress of Jain Community and thus contributing nation building through these educated, resourseful and successful students.

Presently JES has taken a magnanimous project of building a hostel for 120 girls and center for learning at Ghatkopar(Mumbai) to be known as "ARUNIKA" (Shri Arunkumar R Mehta Centre Of Learning).

For more details refer to the projects of JES.
